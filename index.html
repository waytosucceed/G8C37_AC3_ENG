Tenses : Future Perfect and Future Perfect Continuous Tense

The future perfect tense focuses on the completion of an action by a specific time in the future, such as "By noon, I will have completed the project," meaning the project will be finished by that time.

In contrast, the future perfect continuous tense emphasises the duration of an action that will still be in progress up to a future point, like "By noon, I will have been working on the project for five hours," highlighting how long the action will have been ongoing before that time.

Words that I Learnt from the Story

Relentlessly:
Meaning: In a persistent or determined manner, without giving up or showing any signs of stopping.
Example: He pursued his goals relentlessly, working late into the night every day.

Resilience:
Meaning: The ability to recover quickly from difficulties or setbacks.
Example: The team's resilience after their initial defeat helped them win the championship.

Testament:
Meaning: A strong evidence or proof of something.
Example: His calm behaviour during the crisis was a testament to his leadership skills.

Unyielding:
Meaning: Stubborn or resolute; not willing to change or be swayed.
Example: Despite facing numerous obstacles, her unyielding spirit kept her going forward.
